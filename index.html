Features How it can help you

A proffesional support ticket system built on the Twitter Bootstrap framework.
Responsive layout and clean design for those who like their clients to get easily in touch with them and fully satisfy them.

  • Automatic Email report
  • clean and organize tickets viewer
  • Tickets priority option

  • Smart tickets with 4 statuses: Open, Close, Pending, Replied
  • admin panel
  • add admins with different roles
  • Department/role managment system

  • week/month Summary of how you have supported your clients

Installation How to Install the Template

All you need is inside the “TicketSystem” folder but before uploading the files on your server, open up TicketSystem/admin/config.php file with your favorite text editor and insert your database info (I recommend to create a new database for support ticket system on your server) and the first page of ticket information (some info such as your company name and some descriptions for your customers)

You should have created a database and insert its information where is specified in TicketSystem/admin/config.php file. Different hosts have different approaches on Database creation, if you don’t know how to create one, you may ask your host provider support. you may also read here if you want to know more about database creation!
After editing the PHP file, just upload everything inside the “TicketSystem” folder on to a folder on your hosting server, http://domain.com/support/ using a FTP client software like filezilla.

× CONSIDERATION: You can run your template on your computer and edit and preview it offline then upload it to your online server too by installing a server on your computer. Like WampServer or anything else that works for you then view the TicketSystem/index.php through that in a server like environment. Opening the TicketSystem/index.php in your browser without a server installed won’t show you the content of your template completely.
After you have uploaded the files to your server, for example you have uploaded them to http://domain.com/support/ now open http://domain.com/support/install/index.php on your browser to install the ticket system on your server and register as an administrator :)

× NOTE: After installation, I strongly recommend to delete the “install” folder from your server.
NOTE: After installation and setting everything, you need to check if the Captcha code works fine on your server or not as the Captcha code uses securimage for its security. (Catcha code is a free open source code that we have used for our system) so upload Documentation/captcha test/securimage_test.php, or download it from here and upload it anywhere on your server and run it from your browser, if the happy face appears, it means that your server supports all of the requirements, otherwise you will see some errors which means that the Ticket system Captcha code may not work properly on your server, so contact your host provider and let them know about this and the errors you have received, they will take care of this and install the requirements on your server... in most of the servers there shouldn't be any problem, but if your server doesn't have the necessaries, then that's how you get what is needed for the Captcha code to work properly :)
That’s it, enjoy :)

How to get started Let's support clients

After installation you can login from http://domain.com/support/ as an administrator and see your tickets, arranged based on priority and date added, also you can filter your tickets based on their status.
Our new ticket system just made a revolutionary way of how the tickets are organized! tickets have 4 different statuses now:

  • Open tickets, the ones that are added recently and admin didn’t reply them yet
  • Close tickets, are the ones that has been resolved and closed
  • Pending tickets, the ones that admin replied but user again asked a question and it’s still pending to be answered
  • Replied tickets, are the ones that admin answered but the ticket is still open, will be closed after a preiod of time

You can start adding your support departments from the Admins setting / Roles panel.
You can also add new admins with different roles from the Admins setting / Admins panel.

Heads up! Your users can instantly get in touch with you as soon as they come to your support page, they can post a ticket and if they were a new user and didn't sign up for your ticket system yet, they will be automatically signed up and their username and password will be sent to them then they can follow the ticket process.
And finally check how you have supported your clients by checking out the summary panel.
